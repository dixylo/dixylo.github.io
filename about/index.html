About

My name is Xiaofeng LIN. It reads in Mandarin like shjouFUHNG leen or /ɕĭɑʊ˨˩fəŋ˥ lin˧˥/ if you know IPA (For Xiao, insert a very short ee between sh and ou and drop t when you say shout). Xiaofeng is my given name, and Lin my family name, but I usually accept people call me Lin for their convenience (Xiao looks weird and perplexing indeed for English speakers). I sometimes also take Lynn as my English name, which sounds similar to Lin.
I come from Manchuria, which is now the northeastern part of China. I was born and raised in Mukden, the largest city in Manchuria and got my first degree from Jilin University, one of the most famous universities in Manchuria. I had been working at Shanghai Jiao Tong University as a researcher until I realized what I really like, so I quitted my job and came back to college to major in software development. After one and a half years of study, I fortunately graduated from Unitec with a Graduate Diploma in Computing, and am now looking for a job as a programmer.
